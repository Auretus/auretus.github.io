About Me

I'm a secular transhumanist, a SF&F enthusiast, player of games both electronic and tabletop, and an unrepentant technophile. My pronouns are he/him, and I identify as a cisgender male.

Born in the desert wastes of southern California some three dozen years ago, I spent my formative years striving to understand the world around me—a pursuit that has never ceased since. Migrating to Florida with my family, I adapted to the slightly cooler (though much wetter) climate only under protest. Retreating from the unblinking gaze of the daystar, I dedicated myself to intellectual pursuits, specifically ones that would allow me to stay out of the heat as much as possible.

My primary foci of interest center around technology, discovery, and playful exploration. I build computers for myself and my friends, engaging in mild-to-moderate hardware modding where needed to achieve the desired result. I have a strong interest in computer games of many sorts, with a special focus on Roleplaying games. Tabletop roleplaying games are another passion of mine, and I have both participated in and run dozens of games over the years. I greatly enjoy creative visual storytelling, and have a special passion for anime and manga. I also enjoy well-written speculative fiction, with my favorite stories being ones that serve equally well as entertainment and education.

I live underneath one of the primary inbound glidepaths for Orlando International Airport, close enough that trying to enjoy a nap—or even an uninterrupted conversation—in my back yard is an exercise in futility most days.

My dream job is something that lets me bend my talents with technology toward the goal of promoting the flourishing of sapient life across the planet, across the solar system, and beyond (if I get really lucky).
